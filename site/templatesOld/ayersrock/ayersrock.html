Ayers Rock...

by Fabian Stagun
...Ayers Rock, or now called Uluru, is a mountain located
in the central outback of the northern territory.
It lies 335 km (208 mi) south-west of the nearest large
town, Alice Springs. The Mountain is 348 metres tall,
topping the Eiffel Tower by 48m and the Chrysler
Building in NY by 37m. It's consists of arkose, a
coarse-grained sandstone rich in feldspar. The sandy
sediment forming this arkose eroded form big granite
mountains. These mountains also created gravel consisting
mostly of granite and basalt. Uluru also houses a
surprisingly large amount of animals, about 46 to be exact.
To these animals, you can count Dingos, Red Kangaroos,
Spinifex Hopping Mice and others that have developed
to the harsh climate of the outback.
This mountain is sacred to the Pitjantjatjara,
the Aboriginal tribe near this mountain.
Also, the formation was declared a
UNESCO world heritage site and is home to many
ancient paintings, plenty of
springs waterholes and rock caves.

Uluru's Wildlife

Hiking to Uluru, the famous mountains is one of
the best ideas when visiting Australia.

Being famous for its harsh desert bursting
with red and orange colours amid the dry land
the Uluru is soaring up from Australias ground.
Being so big the Ayers rock also is home for a
fascinating amount of animals and plants being
unique to this area. Animals like the Desert
Dingo or the Thorny Devil had to adapt to the
warm and dry climate, and with 46 native mammals,
178 species of birds plus 76 reptiles it has a
lot to offer for enthusiasts, tourists and
observers. To go into a bit more detail about
the animals were now taking a closer look at
the Thorny Devil and the Spinifex Pigeon.



Spinifex Pigeon


The Spinifex Pigeon is one of the endemic species
in Australia, and also listed by the
IUCN (International Union for Conservation of Nature).
This bird lives in rocky hill terrain and prefers dry
climate. It's a small bird with brown plumage and thin
black bars on its back and wings. The food of this
animal is seeds and herbs from the Spinifex triodia
species. They need to drink regularly, that is, for this
reason, occur close to permanent water sources. They have
to live there because the water gained from their food is
to low, but still, need to maintain a significant water
balance because of the high ambient air temperature.
These pigeons can live in the climate due to their low
body heat production. The breeding season of the Spinifex
Pigeon is from August to January. The female lays two
eggs nesting on the ground.


Thorny Devil


The Thorny Devil, also known as the thorny dragon, is a small lizard.
It can age up to 20 years old up growth 8inches long. They feed in the
morning- and afternoon hours because the sun is colder. During noon
they warm themself up in the sun. These animals are heliothermic, which
means they need an outside source of heat because agility and body heat
depends on the outside temperature.

Thanks to microscopic grooves being present all over their body and spike
they can drink water collected from vegetation and tiny droplets in the air.
This ability is adding an excellent adaption to this dry and hot climate.
Because females need to produce eggs, they are more significant. The mating
season is from August to September and the hatching time can endure 3-4 month.
Not until a Thorny Devil is about three he is mature until then he concentrates
on surviving.

The primary defence of this reptile is the spikes on his back and a little
false head on its back consisting of fatty tissue. When a predator tries to
attack it, a Thorny Devil puts its head between his legs showing his fake head.
When a predator, like a bird, now tries to swallow the Thorny Devil, it will
have problems an eventually spit him out again. But all this only if he gets
spotted because this animal owns a camouflage system. When warm, his shell and
skin are red to pale yellow and if cold its dark olive green.
